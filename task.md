## Design Context

### Users
College professors and academic staff who are using this Human Resource Management system to check their leave balances, update their settings, and view team details. Their context is likely busy and focused on academics, so the tool must be frictionless, intuitive, and highly functional.

### Brand Personality
Professional, simple, impeccable, and clean. The interface should evoke a sense of calm, organization, and high-end software reliability (similar to macOS or premium enterprise tools).

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: Modern, minimalist, and elegant.
- **Theme**: Both Light and Dark modes are supported, utilizing high-contrast, accessible monochromatic scales with brand colors providing subtle accents.
- **Key Elements**: Glassmorphism (subtle blur overlays), side-by-side structured forms to reduce vertical sprawl, smooth micro-animations on hover/click, and unified single-page scrolling layouts. 

### Design Principles
1. **Simplicity Over Density**: Do not crowd the UI. Use ample whitespace and clear visual hierarchy so professors can find exactly what they need at a glance.
2. **Premium Polish**: Every interaction (hovering, focusing, loading) should feel intentional. Use smooth transitions (`duration-300` to `duration-500`) and soft, modern focus rings (`ring-2 ring-primary/30`).
3. **Familiar Patterns**: Stick to universally understood premium UI patterns (like overlapping profile avatars on gradient banners, or left-aligned labels with right-aligned inputs) rather than reinventing the wheel.
4. **Action Clarity**: Keep primary actions obvious and tuck secondary/destructive actions (like "Remove Photo") away neatly into subtle states or secondary buttons.
